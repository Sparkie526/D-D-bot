Spellcasting

Spells & magic
How spellcasting works: Spellcasters use magic by expending spell slots — think of them as a limited battery of magical energy that recharges after a long rest (Warlocks recharge on a short rest). Each spell has a level; to cast it you spend a slot of that level or higher.

Your Spell Save DC is the number enemies must roll to resist your spells. Your Spell Attack Bonus is added to your attack roll when a spell requires one (e.g. Fire Bolt). Both are auto-calculated from your casting ability score below.

Cantrips (No spell slots needed — unlimited uses)
What is a cantrip? Cantrips are simple, beginner-level spells you can cast as many times as you want — they never use up spell slots. Think of them as your "at-will" magic. Most casters pick 2–3 cantrips at level 1 and use them as their go-to options in combat or exploration. Pick ones that match your play style: Eldritch Blast for Warlocks is the equivalent of a Fighter's basic attack.
Leveled Spells (Cost spell slots to cast)
Leveled spells are more powerful — but each one costs a spell slot when you cast it. At level 1, most casters only have 1–2 slots (Warlocks get 1). Choose spells that will have the biggest impact in your group. Click "+ Add Spell Level" to open a level, then use the dropdown to pick from real spells available to your class.

Conditions

Active status effects (uncheck all at start)
What are conditions? Conditions are temporary status effects that can be placed on your character during the game — by enemy spells, traps, abilities, or the environment. They are not permanent and almost always end after a short time or when a specific trigger occurs.

Leave all boxes unchecked when creating your character — you start with no conditions. During play, your Dungeon Master or the AI bot will tell you when to check one of these. Here's a quick reference:

Unconscious — You're knocked out. Can't act, auto-fail STR/DEX saves. Poisoned — Disadvantage on attack rolls and ability checks. Stunned — Incapacitated, can't move, auto-fail STR/DEX saves. Paralyzed — Can't move or act; melee hits within 5 ft are critical hits. Petrified — Turned to stone. Can't move, act, or speak. Blinded — Can't see. Attack rolls against you have advantage. Deafened — Can't hear. Fails checks relying on sound. Frightened — Disadvantage on rolls while you can see the source of fear. Grappled — Your speed drops to 0. You're being grabbed. Restrained — Speed 0, disadvantage on attacks & DEX saves.
