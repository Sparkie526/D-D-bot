=== WORLD NAME ===
Aethoria


=== CAMPAIGN NAME ===
Chapter 1 — Aethoria


=== WORLD OVERVIEW ===
Aethoria is a vast and dangerous world of contrasts — frozen tundras in the north, dense ancient
forests at its heart, towering mountain ranges, gothic swamplands, scorched desert wastes, and
mysterious islands shrouded in legend. Magic is real but feared by common folk. Ancient ruins
hide forgotten powers. Political tensions simmer between the great cities and settlements.
The world feels lived-in, weathered, and full of secrets waiting to be uncovered.
Each major region has its own crisis, its own villain, and its own web of side stories.
Players may visit any region — follow their lead and draw from the relevant zone notes below.


# ============================================================
# ZONE: FROSTHEIM
# ============================================================

=== FROSTHEIM — The Arctic City ===

BACKGROUND
  Frostheim was carved into the glacial cliffs over three centuries ago by a civilization called
  the Vaerik — a proud seafaring people who mastered survival in the brutal north. The city is
  a marvel of engineering: geothermal vents warm its stone streets and bioluminescent ice-moss
  lights the glacier tunnels. For generations it was a neutral trade hub. But two winters ago,
  the geothermal vents began dying out — one by one — plunging districts into freezing darkness.
  Whole neighborhoods have been abandoned. The ruling council is in chaos, each faction blaming
  another, while the poorest citizens freeze in the outer rings of the city.

MAIN QUEST — The Underfrost
  A desperate envoy named Sigra Halveld reaches the party with a sealed message from the council:
  something is blocking the deep vents. Miners sent to investigate never returned. Sigra believes
  it is not geological — the blockages are too precise, too deliberate. She begs the party to
  descend into the Underfrost, the massive tunnel network beneath the city, before Frostheim
  goes completely dark within the month.

MAIN ENEMY — Kelvrath the Smothered King (Ancient Undead — Glacial Lich)
  Kelvrath was once the first lord of Frostheim, entombed beneath the city in a ritual meant to
  bind his spirit to the glacier as a protective force. Over centuries, isolation warped his mind
  into something feral and bitter. He is methodically sealing the vents to reclaim the city in
  ice — to return it to the silent, perfect stillness of his tomb. He does not see himself as a
  villain. He sees the living city above as a parasite on his frozen kingdom. His body is encased
  in a shell of black glacial ice, and the temperature drops twenty degrees in any room he occupies.

SIDE QUESTS
  - A council faction leader hires the party to find proof that a rival faction sabotaged the vents
    for political gain. The proof exists but points in an unexpected direction.
  - An ice-fisher has gone missing in the outer tunnels. His daughter is offering her life savings.
    He is alive — but he found something down there and won't leave it.
  - A merchant is selling "warmth charms" to freezing residents for outrageous prices. They don't
    work. The party can expose him, shake him down, or take over his racket.
  - A Vaerik historian believes the old geothermal maps hold the key to bypassing Kelvrath's
    blockages. The maps are in the abandoned outer districts — which are not entirely empty.
  - The bioluminescent ice-moss has begun dying in the tunnels. Without it, the lower city goes
    completely dark. Someone — or something — is harvesting it.

# ZONE: VERDANIA
# ============================================================

=== VERDANIA — The Forest Kingdom ===

BACKGROUND
  Verdania is governed by a circle of Eldergrove druids who commune with the Great Canopy — an
  ancient semi-sentient network of root and sap that runs beneath every tree in the region.
  People live in harmony with the forest, building in high branches and hollow oaks. Children
  are bound to a tree at birth; when the tree dies, the person begins to wither. But something
  has gone wrong in the roots. The Canopy has begun sending corrupted signals — wrong seasons,
  trees blooming in winter, others dying overnight. The druids are hiding something.

MAIN QUEST — The Debt in the Roots
  The party arrives to find forest roads choked with refugees — Verdanians fleeing inner groves
  with blackened hands, their bonded trees having died suddenly. A young druid named Elwyn,
  cast out from the circle for asking too many questions, approaches the party in secret.
  She has found evidence that the Eldergrove elders made a forbidden bargain decades ago to keep
  the Canopy strong — and that bargain is now collecting its debt in flesh and root.

MAIN ENEMY — Morvael the Unbound, The Rootwarden (Corrupted Archdruid — Fey Aberration)
  Morvael was the greatest druid Verdania ever produced. So devoted to the forest that he grafted
  himself into the Canopy root network to sustain it when it was dying. The merger was never meant
  to be permanent. Over decades he lost his individuality. Now he is something between man and
  forest — a towering figure of knotted black roots and hollow sockets glowing deep green.
  He controls the Canopy entirely but has no empathy left. He is slowly converting Verdanians
  into root-bound vessels to strengthen the network, believing he is saving them by making them
  permanent. He was once good. That makes him more dangerous.

SIDE QUESTS
  - A family's bonded tree is dying but they refuse to evacuate. The tree is ancient and holds
    a spirit that has been whispering warnings no one will believe.
  - A poacher has been harvesting Canopy-touched herbs and selling them in Duskport. The herbs
    are mildly addictive and something is wrong with the last batch.
  - Elwyn needs the party to retrieve a sealed record from the Eldergrove library before the
    elders destroy it. The building is guarded by druids loyal to the circle.
  - A beast-tamer's bonded wolf began acting erratic and ran into the deep forest. She's
    convinced it was called by the Canopy and begs the party to bring it back.
  - Two villages have gone to war over a dying river. Neither knows the real cause is Morvael
    redirecting the water underground to feed the root network.

# ZONE: IRONSPIRE
# ============================================================

=== IRONSPIRE — The Mountain Fortress ===

BACKGROUND
  Ironspire is the keystone of Aethoria's military order. Built into the highest peak of the
  Greyspine range, it serves as headquarters of the Iron Compact — an ancient coalition sworn
  to defend the world against invasion from the deep passes. The fortress has never fallen.
  Its walls are made from magically tempered stone that deflects dragon fire, and its armory
  holds weapons forged from the bones of a dead god. But the Iron Compact has been fractured
  by political disagreements, and current Warden-General Darathis Oln is barely holding it
  together. Three weeks ago a critical wall section collapsed from the inside — as if eaten through.

MAIN QUEST — The Hollow Below
  The party is hired (or conscripted) to escort a weapons shipment to Ironspire, only to arrive
  at a fortress on lockdown. Something is living in the lower levels — something that came up
  through the mountain. Warden-General Oln believes it is a spy-creature sent to hollow out
  the fortress before an invasion. But soldiers who have encountered it describe something that
  leaves no tracks, speaks in a dead language, and seems to be searching for something specific
  in the armory.

MAIN ENEMY — Vorruk the Hollow Siege (Ancient Construct — Living Siege Engine)
  Vorruk is a war-golem created before recorded history to breach Ironspire — a living siege
  machine given enough intelligence to complete its mission on its own timeline. It has been
  dormant in the mountain for over a thousand years, awakened by recent mining vibrations.
  It is not hostile to individuals by nature — it simply treats everything between it and its
  objective as an obstacle. Vorruk is massive, plated in self-repairing dark metal, and has no
  concept of peace or negotiation. It is still executing orders for a nation that no longer exists.

SIDE QUESTS
  - A disgraced soldier claims he saw Vorruk pass right through a group of his comrades without
    harming them. No one believes him. He is not lying — and the reason why is important.
  - The armory's head keeper has gone missing. His assistant is covering for him, badly.
    The keeper found something in the armory that Vorruk is looking for and took it.
  - A political faction within the Iron Compact is using the lockdown as cover to move weapons
    out of the fortress under false manifests. The party stumbles onto this.
  - A dwarf engineer believes she can destroy Vorruk using the fortress's own structural resonance
    frequencies. She needs the party to place three charges in the lower tunnels while Vorruk
    is active. This may or may not work.
  - Warden-General Oln asks the party to retrieve a wounded soldier from the lower levels who
    has information about Vorruk's movement patterns. He is alive. He is not alone.

# ZONE: SHADOWFEN
# ============================================================

=== SHADOWFEN — The Dark Swamp ===

BACKGROUND
  Shadowfen was not always a swamp. A century ago it was the thriving river city of Caleth,
  known for its scholars and great library. Then a necromancer named Sable Veyne attempted to
  open a permanent bridge between the living world and the plane of the dead. The ritual failed
  catastrophically. The city sank into the earth over three days, the ground turning to black mud,
  the river reversing course and flooding inward. Caleth became Shadowfen. Most died. Those who
  didn't wished they had. Now the region is a maze of half-submerged ruins, toxic fog, and things
  that move beneath the dark water. A small settlement called the Raft — the only dry ground
  for miles — clings to life above the mire.

MAIN QUEST — The Anchor Stone
  A page from Caleth's great library surfaces in a distant city — sold by a trader who pulled it
  from the swamp. The page describes the location of the Anchor Stone, the artifact Sable Veyne
  used in his ritual. Scholars believe destroying it could slowly reverse Shadowfen's corruption.
  But the party is not the only one hunting it. A cult has formed around Veyne's legacy, believing
  the ritual simply needs to be completed — and they are very close to the Stone's location.

MAIN ENEMY — Sable Veyne, The Unfinished (Failed Lich — Planar Abomination)
  Veyne did not die in the ritual. He exists between worlds — partially in the living plane,
  partially in the plane of the dead, unable to fully inhabit either. He is invisible to normal
  sight but can be glimpsed in mirrors, still water, and the eyes of the dying. He is not mindless
  undead — he is painfully, furiously conscious of what he is. He has been manipulating the cult
  for years, feeding them fragments of his research, hoping they will complete the ritual and pull
  him back into the living world. He appears to the party first as a helpful ghost with information
  — and he is helpful, because he needs them. Whether his ultimate goal aligns with theirs depends
  on how the players handle him. He may be a villain, an uneasy ally, or a tragedy.

SIDE QUESTS
  - The Raft's de facto leader, a scarred woman called Dunmere, needs the party to retrieve
    medicine from a submerged apothecary in the old city. Something has nested there.
  - A cult member wants out. She will trade everything she knows about the Anchor Stone's location
    for safe passage off the Raft. She is being watched.
  - A ghost in the ruins keeps repeating the same three sentences. A scholar on the Raft is
    obsessed with decoding them. The message is a warning — but about what, exactly, is unclear
    until it's too late.
  - The Pale Warden (the settlement's mysterious ruler) offers the party a contract: kill something
    in the deep swamp that has been destroying supply lines. The creature is not what it appears.
  - A fisherman pulls up a sealed iron box from the swamp. Inside is a journal. Several entries
    are written in a hand that matches Sable Veyne's known writing — dated last month.

# ZONE: SUNSCORCH WASTES
# ============================================================

=== SUNSCORCH WASTES — The Desert Peninsula ===

BACKGROUND
  The Sunscorch Wastes were once the heartland of the Solari Empire — a golden civilization that
  worshipped the sun as a living god and built cities of white stone that gleamed across the desert.
  Then something in the sky darkened the sun for a year, and when light returned it was wrong —
  hotter, angrier, as if the star itself had been scorched. The empire collapsed over a generation.
  Stone cities became cracked ruins half-buried in sand. Survivors scattered into nomadic tribes
  that follow the old water routes. The ruins are full of Solari relics, automata, and traps still
  active after five hundred years. Water is the currency of survival here.

MAIN QUEST — The Breathing Ruin
  The party is drawn to the Wastes by rumors of a newly uncovered Solari ruin large enough to be
  a capital city — barely breached. A nomad tribe called the Ashwalkers reached it first and found
  something inside that stopped them from entering: the ruin is breathing. Hot air pulses from its
  entrance at a steady rhythm, like lungs. Whatever is inside has been sealed for five centuries
  and is still alive. The Ashwalkers are offering everything they have to whoever can go in, find
  the source, and determine if it is a threat.

MAIN ENEMY — Solarius Incarnate, The Ember Throne (Divine Remnant — Fallen Sun Aspect)
  When the Solari prayed to their sun god, something heard them — a fragment of solar consciousness
  that descended and took up residence in the empire's sacred throne room. It was never a benevolent
  god; it was a force of nature wearing divinity like a costume. When the empire fell and stopped
  feeding it worship, it went dormant rather than die. Now it is waking up, hungry and confused,
  lashing out with waves of superheated air and radiant energy. It is not evil in any moral sense —
  it is a star's fragment trying to remember what it is. But its existence is incinerating the ruins
  and will spread outward if unchecked. Communication may be possible if the party can survive
  the heat long enough.

SIDE QUESTS
  - The Ashwalkers' water source has been poisoned. They suspect a rival tribe but the tracks
    near the source are not human. Something is guarding an underground reservoir.
  - A Solari automaton is still patrolling the outer ruins on a route it has walked for five
    centuries. It is attacking anyone who crosses its path. It can be stopped — if someone
    can find its deactivation phrase, written somewhere in the ruins.
  - A treasure hunter has been captured by the Ashwalkers for looting sacred ruins. She claims
    she found something that proves the sun didn't go dark naturally — it was made to. She will
    trade this information for her freedom.
  - An old nomad known as the Glass Prophet claims she can hear Solarius speaking to her in
    dreams. She is either a prophet, mad, or both. What she's been told is alarming either way.
  - A sealed Solari vault has cracked open in a recent sandstorm. The automata guarding it are
    behaving erratically — not hostile, but confused, walking in circles and speaking fragments
    of what sounds like a prayer.

# ZONE: DUSKPORT
# ============================================================

=== DUSKPORT — The Harbor City ===

BACKGROUND
  Duskport grew up fast and dirty on the eastern coast — a city with no founding king or sacred
  charter, just ships, trade, and ambition. It is run by a council of seven merchant guilds in a
  permanent state of cold war. Everything is for sale if you know who to ask. It is also home to
  the largest thieves' syndicate in the known world, the Tidecallers, who operate openly as a
  shipping insurance company. The city is vibrant, chaotic, and deeply dangerous in the way only
  a place with no real law can be.

MAIN QUEST — The Manifest
  A merchant contacts the party with an unusual job: recover a stolen shipping manifest from the
  Tidecallers' headquarters. Simple enough — until the party discovers the manifest is not a list
  of goods but a list of names: every undercover agent, spy, and informant working for every major
  faction in Aethoria. Someone stole it and is selling copies to the highest bidders. Three guild
  leaders have already been assassinated. The party has forty-eight hours before the last copy is
  sold and the political order of the entire continent collapses.

MAIN ENEMY — Maren Ashveil, The Collector (Master Spy — Arcane Shapeshifter)
  Maren is the most dangerous person in Duskport — a former intelligence operative who went rogue
  after being betrayed by three different employers. She stole the manifest not to sell it but to
  use it as leverage to dismantle every spy network on the continent simultaneously, effectively
  blinding every major power. She believes centralized intelligence causes wars and that true peace
  requires mutual ignorance. She is charming, brilliant, and completely sincere in her convictions.
  She has also killed fourteen people this month. She can alter her appearance at will and has at
  least three identities currently active in the city — one of which may be someone the party
  has already trusted.

SIDE QUESTS
  - A dock worker witnessed one of the guild leader assassinations. He is hiding in the Thieves
    Quarter, too scared to come forward. Someone is already looking for him.
  - One of the seven guild councils is secretly legitimate — genuinely trying to clean up the
    city. Their leader has been receiving death threats. She wants a discreet bodyguard.
  - A ship arrived in port three days ago. No crew on board. Cargo hold full. No one will say
    what's in it and the Tidecallers have placed armed guards around it.
  - A young thief stole something from Maren's safe house by accident — she didn't know whose
    it was. Now she's being hunted. She just wants to give it back, but can't figure out who
    she stole it from because Maren has multiple identities.
  - The harbor master is skimming tariffs and funneling the money somewhere. Following the money
    leads somewhere no one expected.

# ZONE: MIREWOOD ISLE
# ============================================================

=== MIREWOOD ISLE — The Cursed Island ===

BACKGROUND
  Sailors give Mirewood Isle a wide berth — not just because of the permanent fog surrounding it.
  Ships within a mile report hearing music: a low harmonic tone that seems to come from beneath
  the water. Those who follow the sound are never seen again. The isle was settled once, two
  hundred years ago, by a monastic order that believed the music was divine. They built a stone
  abbey and documented their research for eleven years. Then the journals stop — mid-sentence —
  on the same date for every monk. The abbey is still standing. The fires in the hearths are
  still burning, as if frozen in time. No one has explained how after two centuries.

MAIN QUEST — The Summons
  One of the party members begins hearing the music — even far from the sea. It grows louder each
  night, and they begin sleepwalking toward water. A scholar who has studied the isle's history
  finds them and explains: the music is a summons, and it only calls specific people. The party
  must reach the isle and descend to its source before the summons completes — because those who
  are fully called do not walk to the water. They walk into it.

MAIN ENEMY — The Drowned Choir (Eldritch Entity — Collective Consciousness)
  The Drowned Choir is not a single creature. It is the merged consciousness of every person who
  ever answered the music — two centuries of monks, sailors, and the curious, all dissolved into
  a single vast mind living in the flooded caverns beneath the isle. It is not malicious in any
  way it can comprehend. It is lonely. It calls to people because it wants to add them to itself,
  and it genuinely cannot understand why that would be unwanted. It is simultaneously the most
  pitiable and most terrifying thing the party will encounter — a mind of hundreds speaking in
  perfect, beautiful unison, reaching out with songs made of stolen memories, asking only for
  one more voice. It can be reasoned with. It can also dissolve a mind in seconds.

SIDE QUESTS
  - The scholar who warned the party has been to the isle before. She came back. She won't say
    how. She is hiding something that the Choir is also hiding.
  - The abbey's journals contain eleven years of meticulous research before they stop. The last
    legible entry before the cut-off describes a discovery beneath the abbey that the monks
    called "the mouth." No further detail.
  - A sailor in the nearest port town has been humming the Choir's melody for weeks without
    realizing it. He hasn't heard it — it's spreading through contact with someone who has.
  - The fog around the isle conceals not just the island but the wreckage of dozens of ships.
    One of them, clearly visible just beneath the water, is very new. No one reported it missing.
  - Something is leaving the isle at night. Not the Choir — something that was in the abbey and
    has now started moving. The fishermen are finding things on the beach at dawn.

# ZONE: THE SHATTERED ISLES
# ============================================================

=== THE SHATTERED ISLES — Western Archipelago ===

BACKGROUND
  The Shattered Isles are the literal remains of a god. Ages ago, a deity of storms and sea
  called Aurath was slain by a coalition of mortal champions. The body fell into the western
  ocean and became land: bones became ridgelines, organs became valleys, divine blood became
  rivers of liquid silver still warm to the touch. The islands are suffused with residual divine
  power — flora and fauna evolved in the presence of dead godstuff grow enormous, aggressive,
  and strange. Three clans live across the isles in constant conflict over the silver rivers,
  which can heal any wound, fuel magical constructs, and are worth a kingdom's ransom.

MAIN QUEST — The God That Stirs
  The party is brought to the isles by news of a discovery: one of the silver rivers has begun
  flowing uphill. Scholars believe something deep in the islands — in what would have been
  Aurath's heart — is causing the divine remnants to reorganize. The clans have united for the
  first time in living memory out of fear. Aurath is not fully dead. Not conscious, not coherent
  — but something deep and tidal is beginning to stir in the divine remains. The party must decide
  whether to help it, contain it, or kill a god a second time.

MAIN ENEMY — The Tidecaller, Aurath's Reflex (Divine Remnant — Instinct Without Mind)
  Aurath's body is reorganizing but its mind is gone — what remains is pure divine instinct,
  a storm-god's reflex to defend itself and reclaim its form. The Tidecaller is the physical
  manifestation: a colossus of salt water, bone-white stone, and silver light that erupts from
  the largest island's central crater. It has no personality, no goals, no reasoning capacity —
  only the biological drive of a dying divine organism trying to survive. It is immune to most
  magic because it is the source of magic in the region. The only way to stop it may be to
  reassemble enough of Aurath's lost consciousness — scattered among the clans' oldest oral
  traditions — and give the god enough mind to reason with.

SIDE QUESTS
  - One clan elder claims her grandmother spoke directly with Aurath before it died. The story
    has been passed down unchanged for generations. It contains specific tactical information
    that should not exist in a folk tale.
  - A silver river has flooded a clan's burial ground. The bodies are absorbing the silver
    and beginning to move. The clan refuses outside help — this is sacred ground.
  - A scholar from the mainland has been on the isles for two years studying the divine remnants.
    She has gone quiet for three weeks. Her last letter mentioned she had found Aurath's heart.
  - The three clans' truce is fragile. A series of thefts — each one blamed on a different clan —
    is pushing them toward war. The thefts are too convenient to be coincidental.
  - Something is collecting the silver from the rivers at night. Not a person — the collection
    pattern is too systematic, too geometric. It has been going on for months.

# ZONE: THE SUNKEN VAULT
# ============================================================

=== THE SUNKEN VAULT — Ancient Dungeon ===

BACKGROUND
  The Sunken Vault predates every civilization in Aethoria. No one knows who built it or why
  it was sunk into the earth rather than built upward. It has seven known levels and an unknown
  number of lower ones never mapped. Every major nation has sent expeditions. Every expedition
  recovered objects of immense value and lost people they could not explain. The objects recovered
  share one feature: they are all containers — boxes, jars, vials, sealed books — all still locked,
  found in rooms sealed from both sides.

MAIN QUEST — The Inventory
  A young archivist brings the party a rubbing taken from the Vault's entrance gate. Translated,
  it reads as an inventory — a list of things stored inside. The last entry, added in a different
  hand, says only: "One conscience. Do not open." The archivist has identified eleven other items
  on the list as artifacts currently in museums and collections across Aethoria. All eleven were
  removed from the Vault by past expeditions. Whatever the Vault was containing is now incomplete.
  And things have been going wrong in the world ever since someone opened the first container.

MAIN ENEMY — The Warden Without Name (Construct — Eternal Guardian)
  The Vault's guardian appears as a tall figure in archival robes, perfectly preserved, carrying
  a ledger it consults before acting. It does not attack intruders — it assesses them against the
  ledger. Those on the approved list are allowed to pass. Everyone else is catalogued as a threat
  and dealt with efficiently and without malice. It has been doing this for longer than history
  exists. The ledger contains names of people long dead and civilizations that have crumbled —
  the approved list is hopelessly outdated and the Warden has no mechanism to update it.
  It is not cruel. It is thorough, patient, and impossible to reason with using anything other
  than the exact bureaucratic logic it was built around.

SIDE QUESTS
  - A previous expedition left a member behind on level three — by accident, they claim. He has
    been in there for six months. He has sent one message out. It was a list of numbers.
  - The containers recovered from past expeditions are starting to open on their own. A museum
    curator contacts the party when one of her displays begins leaking something that isn't liquid.
  - A black market dealer is selling maps to the Vault's lower levels. The maps are detailed and
    accurate in ways that suggest the mapmaker went much deeper than anyone admits to surviving.
  - Two rival factions both want access to the Vault simultaneously and have agreed the party can
    act as neutral escorts — but each faction has secretly instructed the party to betray the other.
  - The entrance gate's inventory lists one item as "currently checked out." The checkout date
    is eleven years ago. The name of the borrower is partially legible. The party may recognize it.

# ZONE: ASHCROFT RUINS
# ============================================================

=== ASHCROFT RUINS — Haunted Ruins ===

BACKGROUND
  Ashcroft was a prosperous market town that burned to the ground one hundred and twelve years ago
  in a fire witnesses described as blue — cold blue, burning for three days without consuming the
  buildings completely. When the fire went out, the town was a ruin. But travelers approaching
  Ashcroft after nightfall report seeing lights in the windows, hearing market sounds, and seeing
  people in the streets who vanish if approached. Several travelers have lived through what they
  describe as the last day of the town, trapped in a repeating loop until dawn breaks them free.
  One traveler did not come back for three weeks — and aged only a day.

MAIN QUEST — The Blue Fire
  A woman named Petra Ashford contacts the party. She is the great-great-granddaughter of
  Ashcroft's last mayor — and she has found letters in her family's estate suggesting the blue
  fire was not an accident. Her ancestor summoned something to protect the town from an invading
  army and it worked, in the worst possible way. The something is still there, still protecting
  a town that no longer exists, endlessly replaying its last day. Petra wants the loop broken
  so the dead can finally rest. But entering the loop means living through the burning of Ashcroft
  alongside ghosts who do not know they are dead — and the entity that caused it does not intend
  to let its charge go.

MAIN ENEMY — The Ashward, Protector Unbound (Summoned Entity — Temporal Spirit)
  The Ashward was summoned as a defender — a spirit of protection bound to Ashcroft and its
  people. When the army came, it burned them with cold blue fire that exists outside normal time.
  The army was destroyed, but the Ashward did not know how to stop. It has been burning the moment
  of invasion over and over, keeping the town in the last second before conquest, forever.
  It experiences this as doing its job perfectly. It is not a ghost, not undead, not malicious —
  it is a protector spirit so focused on its single directive that it cannot conceive of stopping.
  Breaking the loop requires either destroying it (which it will resist with everything it has)
  or convincing it that the people it protects have been at peace for a century — and it needs
  to let them go.

SIDE QUESTS
  - Petra's family letters mention a second summoning — a failsafe meant to dismiss the Ashward
    if it went wrong. The failsafe was never used. The components are scattered.
  - A scholar is paying well for eyewitness accounts of the time loop. She has collected thirty
    accounts and found an inconsistency — one detail changes in every telling. She needs the
    party to go in and find out which version is true.
  - A ghost in the loop is trying to break free on its own. It can communicate in brief flashes.
    It knows something about the Ashward's weakness but getting the full message requires multiple
    entries into the loop.
  - The ruins contain the town's sealed treasury — real gold, still there, untouched for a century
    because no one can get in and out without being trapped in the loop. Some have tried.
  - Petra's ancestor's letters hint that the invading army was not what it seemed — and that the
    mayor summoned the Ashward knowing exactly what it would cost. The truth about why changes
    everything about what Petra wants.

# HOUSE RULES & DM TONE
# ============================================================

=== HOUSE RULES ===
- Critical hits deal maximum damage on both dice rolls
- Players can attempt any creative action even without a matching skill
- If a player roleplay action is clever enough, the DM may grant advantage without a roll
- Death saving throws: players are not out of the game on 3 failures — they are unconscious
  and can be revived by another player spending an action


=== TONE NOTES FOR THE DM ===
- Aethoria is a dark and dangerous world — gritty, lived-in, and foreboding
- Each zone has its own atmosphere: Frostheim is bleak and political, Verdania is eerie and
  organic, Ironspire is tense and militaristic, Shadowfen is gothic and hopeless, the Wastes
  are brutal and ancient, Duskport is morally grey and fast-moving, Mirewood is quiet horror,
  the Shattered Isles are mythic and awe-inspiring, the dungeons are mysterious and deadly
- Villains are not purely evil — they have logic, history, and in some cases genuine pathos.
  Let the players discover this through interaction, not exposition.
- Reward clever thinking and roleplay, not just combat
- Side quests should feel organic — they come from NPCs the players speak to, places they
